<p><p>Membrane technology in the pharma, biopharma, and life sciences sectors involves various filtration processes to separate and purify substances. Microfiltration removes large particles, while ultrafiltration targets smaller molecules, such as proteins. Nanofiltration is used for selectively removing ionic and small organic compounds, and reverse osmosis effectively purifies water by filtering out nearly all impurities. Other technologies include forward osmosis and membrane distillation, which enhance separation efficiencies and expand applications in drug formulation, bioprocessing, and quality control.</p></p>
