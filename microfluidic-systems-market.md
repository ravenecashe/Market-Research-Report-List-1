<p><p>The microfluidic systems market is categorized into three main types: glass, polymer, and silicon. Glass microfluidics offer excellent optical properties and chemical resistance, making them ideal for analytical applications. Polymer microfluidics, often fabricated using materials like PDMS, are favored for their flexibility, cost-effectiveness, and ease of prototyping, catering to disposable applications. Silicon microfluidics, known for their precision and integration capabilities, are mainly used in high-performance devices, particularly in biomedical and electronic applications, owing to their compatibility with advanced manufacturing techniques.</p></p>
