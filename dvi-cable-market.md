<p><p>DVI cables come in three primary types: DVI-A, DVI-D, and DVI-I. DVI-A (analog) transmits only analog signals, suitable for connection to analog displays. DVI-D (digital) handles only digital signals, providing higher quality for digital monitors. DVI-I (integrated) supports both analog and digital signals, offering flexibility for various devices. The market for these cables caters to different technological needs, from legacy systems to modern displays, ensuring compatibility across a wide range of electronic devices.</p></p>
