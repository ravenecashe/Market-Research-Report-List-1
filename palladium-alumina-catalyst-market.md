<p><p>The palladium alumina catalyst market is divided into two primary types: powder and granular. Powder catalysts are fine particles that offer high surface area and increased reactivity, making them suitable for applications requiring rapid chemical reactions. In contrast, granular catalysts consist of larger, uniform particles that provide enhanced mechanical strength and improved flow characteristics, making them ideal for fixed-bed reactors. Both forms are essential for various industrial processes, including hydrogenation, dehydrogenation, and pollution control.</p></p>
