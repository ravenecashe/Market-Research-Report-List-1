<p><p>3D bio-printers in the medical market utilize various techniques for tissue engineering and regenerative medicine. Magnetic 3D bioprinting uses magnetic fields to control cell placement. Laser-assisted bioprinting employs laser energy to precisely deposit biomaterials. Inkjet 3D bioprinting ejects droplets of bio-ink onto substrates, allowing for high-resolution structures. Microextrusion 3D bioprinting pushes bio-inks through fine nozzles, enabling consistent layering. Each method offers unique advantages in creating complex tissue structures for medical applications.</p></p>
