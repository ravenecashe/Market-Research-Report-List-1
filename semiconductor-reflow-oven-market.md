<p><ul><li>Convection Ovens</li><li>Vapour Phase Ovens</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The semiconductor reflow oven market includes various types of equipment used for soldering components onto printed circuit boards. Convection ovens utilize hot air circulation to evenly heat the surfaces, providing precise control over temperature profiles. In contrast, vapor phase ovens utilize vaporized solvents that condense on the PCB, achieving uniform heating without direct airflow, which minimizes oxidation. Both types cater to different manufacturing needs, affecting quality, efficiency, and cost, thereby impacting production line decisions in the semiconductor industry.</p></p>
