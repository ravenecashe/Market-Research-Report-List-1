<p><p>Inorganic flame retardants are chemical compounds used to inhibit or suppress the combustion of materials, primarily in polymers, textiles, and construction materials. These retardants work by releasing non-flammable gases, forming protective char layers, or absorbing heat during combustion. Common types include aluminum hydroxide, magnesium hydroxide, antimony trioxide, and boron-based compounds. They are preferred for their low toxicity, environmental friendliness, and effectiveness in high-temperature applications.</p><p>The Inorganic Flame Retardants Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 4.9% during the forecast period. This growth is driven by increasing demand from industries such as construction, electronics, and automotive, where fire safety regulations are becoming stricter. The rising awareness of fire hazards and the need for sustainable, non-toxic solutions are also contributing to market expansion.</p><p>Recent trends in the market include the development of halogen-free flame retardants due to environmental and health concerns, as well as advancements in nanotechnology to enhance the efficiency of inorganic retardants. Additionally, the Asia-Pacific region is emerging as a key market due to rapid industrialization and urbanization, coupled with stringent fire safety norms. Overall, the market is poised for steady growth, supported by innovation and regulatory compliance.</p></p>
