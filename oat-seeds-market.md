<p><p>The oat seeds market primarily consists of two types: common seeds and naked oats. Common seeds feature a hull that encases the grain, providing protection but requiring processing to remove the outer layer before consumption. In contrast, naked oats are hull-less, making them easier to prepare and often preferred for their nutritional benefits. Both types serve diverse applications, from livestock feed to human consumption, catering to increasing demand for healthy grains and specialty diets in the agricultural market.</p></p>
